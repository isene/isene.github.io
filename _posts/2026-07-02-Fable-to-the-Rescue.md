On my quest to [own my software](/2026/04/MyTools.html), I am in the process of adding an X server to [CHasm](https://isene.org/chasm/). That means reducing the 4M lines of code in [X11](https://www.x.org/) to the exact set of functionality I need in a fraction of the size and in pure Assembly. I spent the weekend getting a functional X server running but then hit a wall. After getting [GIMP](https://www.gimp.org/) to run on [frame](https://github.com/isene/frame), there were serious issues with getting menus and popup windows working. And for three evenings I kept [Claude Code](https://claude.com/claude-code) Opus 4.8 banging its virtual head against that wall in the hope of smashing through. Tons of prompts, lots of angles. But no luck.

Then we got [Fable 5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-fable-5-mythos-5) back online, and with a few hours tonight the wall dissipated. GIMP is running nicely, tray icons in [strip](https://github.com/isene/tile) are showing and are functional. It seems I will be getting my own X server after all. Say what you will, but Fable is pretty fab.
