Have you ever read through a 300-page book only to flip the last page thinking, "Damn, they could have written the main points in a one-page summary so I didn't have to read the other 299 pages of anecdotes."?

I have written several normal-sized books. And I've read lots of books that I thought should have been compressed down to one page. The same with my collegue/best friend [Brendan Martin](http://brendanmartin.com), which is why we decided to start publishing OnePageBooks (1PBs) on key subjects. Presented here in the order of general interest:
